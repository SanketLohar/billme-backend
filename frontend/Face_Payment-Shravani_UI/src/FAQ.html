frequently asked questions
How does Face Pay recognise me?
We use 3D infrared depth mapping combined with more than 100 unique facial landmarks. Your faceprint is encrypted and never leaves your device during authentication – it’s matched against the secure token stored in your wallet.
Can I use a photo or mask to pay?
No. Our liveness detection (eye blink, micro-movements, texture analysis) prevents spoofing using photos, videos or masks. It’s compliant with global biometric security standards (ISO 30107-3).
What if I change my appearance (glasses, beard)?
Face Pay adapts gradually. Small changes are fine – the system uses core geometric ratios. For drastic changes, we may ask you to quickly verify via PIN once, then it updates your template.
Is my face data stored on company servers?
Never. Your face features are transformed into an irreversible mathematical hash stored only on your secure element. We follow privacy-by-design – you control the data.
Which devices support Face Pay?
Any smartphone with a front‑facing camera (iOS 15+/Android 11+) and our app. For optimal security we recommend devices with a dedicated biometric co-processor.

guidelines & best practices

Enrollment environment

Make sure you're in even lighting – avoid strong backlight or shadows on your face. Remove sunglasses and masks during initial registration.

98% success rate

Privacy protection

Never share your face verification selfies or codes. Face Pay operators will never ask you to “send a video of your face”.

  • only authenticate inside the official app
  • check the green secure badge

One face per account

For security, each Face Pay account can be bound to only one active face template. Family sharing uses delegated access tokens, not face sharing.

Quick payment tips

Look directly at the camera from 30–50 cm. Transactions complete under 2 seconds. For high-value payments (>€100) occasional PIN re-check adds extra safety.

Lost or stolen device

Immediately suspend Face Pay via web dashboard or by calling support. Biometric data remains encrypted; without your live face it’s useless.
